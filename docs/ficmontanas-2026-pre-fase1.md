## SINOPSIS EN — TODOS LOS CONFIRMADOS

### FICCIÓN
**Refracción** (LB): "Eloísa, a 20-year-old girl, is in two relationships at the same time. One night, after having been with both girls, she feels overwhelmed by living in this situation of deceit, and in the middle of her confusion she goes out to ride her bike through the streets of Medellín."

**Kanekalon** (LB): "Teyo, a young trans woman, prepares to enter her new female school. In the middle of a trip through Quibdó, a hot city of sound, rhythms and glances that she faces before entering class converge."

**La gran hazaña** (LB): "A rescue with a spinning tops, feathers, eggs and friends."

**Sukua** (LB): "When a Kogui child is given a water pistol as a toy, he begins to explore the familiar spaces of his territory with a new look."

**Lo que se guarda** (LB): "Karla (15) juggles the weight of caring for her sick father, her neurodivergent younger brother, and keeping their home afloat. But when a chance to stop the daily bullying her brother faces presents itself, she decides it's time to act."

**Época de Plagas** (LB): "While her father is away, a girl hears strange noises from her sick mother's room. As nature and humans suffer from foreign waste, she longs to see her mother—but is she ready for what's behind the door?"

**Favela Amarela** (LB): "Raised in the favelas of Rio de Janeiro, Damião joins a local drug gang to help pay for college. During a night shift, he spots hooded figures sneaking through the forest toward a church."

**A las 9 es la cita** (Feelsales): "Between love and his past, a taxi driver with a double life struggles with his reality, unleashing a dance of secrets and decisions that change his destiny."

### ANIMACIÓN
**Emechiche** (LB): "The Colombian Montes de María are home to the emechiche (Saguinus oedipus). An endangered species, we will observe their complex relationship with their young and their environment. We will analyse their survival, allowing us to witness their feelings and wild instincts."

**Tuktu, Padre Maíz** (LB): "In the mountains of Colombia, Juan, a 13-year-old indigenous peasant boy, is forced to flee from the monster of war. In his escape, between the whistling of bullets and the guidance of his ancestors, he awakens an ancestral power that transforms him into Tuktu: a Taita of extraordinary strength."

### DOCUMENTAL
**Sombras en la niebla** (LB): "High in a páramo in Colombia, the sheep that Edualdo and Ana have tended for decades graze across the land. Amid the fog and the demands of herding, their youngest son, Will, grows up between childhood play and the rhythms of rural life."

### NATURALEZA
**Armadillos, la voz bajo el suelo** (El Espectador): "In the vast savannas of Colombia's Eastern Plains, the armadillo guides us through an almost invisible world. From the intimacy of its tunnels, this ancient inhabitant of the llano narrates the history of its territory, revealing the ecological richness of morichales, savannas, and the delicate balance sustaining countless species. Through its voice, it shares its role as nature's architect, while observing how its home transforms through human expansion, fire, and habitat loss. In this scenario, an unexpected encounter between science and community emerges: farmers, scientists, and indigenous peoples who begin to listen to the 'voice beneath the soil'."

### LARGOMETRAJES
**Un poeta** (LB/Wikipedia): "An aging poet finds purpose mentoring Yurlady, a talented teen, though exposing her to the poetry scene might be unwise."

**Neurótica Anónima** (LB): "The story of Iluminada, a woman who, having studied to be a literature teacher, ends up working as an usher in an iconic cinema in Havana."

**Lactar** (LB): "A 62-year-old woman, trapped in a life of empty privilege, becomes pregnant by her driver. Facing rejection from her family and society, she decides to fight to bring to life a baby that seems impossible at her late age."

**Herencia: Los Cantos de la Tierra** (LB): "In the hands of the peasant, the countryside becomes a productive space; everything around it bears fruit, not only the land, but also the cultural expressions born from its most intimate experiences."

**La hija del cóndor** (LB/web): "A young Quechua midwife sings to pregnant women, soothing their pain. When she decides to leave for the city, the community begins to question her path."
